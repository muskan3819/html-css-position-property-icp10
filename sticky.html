Here's some information about WhatsApp:

What it is:-
WhatsApp is a free, cross-platform messaging app that allows users to send text, photos, videos, documents, and location, as well as make voice and video calls.

How it works:-
WhatsApp uses a Wi-Fi connection to communicate, making it cost-effective and popular with users who don't have unlimited data plans. It can be accessed on mobile devices and computers.

Who created it:-
WhatsApp was founded in 2009 by Brian Acton and Jan Koum, who previously worked at Yahoo. Facebook acquired WhatsApp in 2014 for approximately US$19.3 billion.

How to use it:-
To register for WhatsApp, you need a cellular mobile phone number. You can: Select your country from the drop-down list Enter your phone number in international phone number format

Tap Done or Next:-
Tap Ok to receive your 6-digit registration code via SMS or phone call Enter your 6-digit code to complete registration

Privacy:-
WhatsApp has a Privacy Policy that explains how it collects and uses information, including how it shares information with third parties. You can request more information about WhatsApp's data practices.
